"O brawling love, O loving hate"
Theme: Conflict, emotions, youth
This oxymoron shows Romeo's confusion about love and the intense emotions of young people.
"I defy you, stars!"
Theme: Fate, destiny, tragedy
Romeo challenges fate itself, showing his desperation and the inevitability of his tragic end.
"My only love sprung from my only hate."
Theme: Forbidden love, family conflict
Juliet realizes the cruel irony that she loves her family's enemy. Shows the power of love vs. duty.
"O happy dagger!"
Theme: Tragedy, desperation, death
Juliet's final words before suicide. The oxymoron "happy dagger" shows her desperation and tragic fate.

Theme: Feud consequences, blame
Mercutio's dying curse on both families. Shows the devastating consequences of their hatred.
"Ancient grudge"
Theme: Long-term family conflict, violence
From the Prologue. Shows the feud is old and deep-rooted, setting up the tragedy to come.

PEEZ Structure

P ‚Äì Make a clear Point that answers the question.

E ‚Äì Use a short quote as Evidence.

E ‚Äì Explain what the quote shows.

Z ‚Äì Zoom in on one word or technique (noun, verb, metaphor, foreshadowing, etc.).

Example Paragraph (Theme: Fate)

Shakespeare presents fate as an unavoidable force controlling the lovers. Romeo declares, "I defy you, stars!", suggesting he believes destiny has trapped him. The noun "stars" symbolises fate as something powerful and distant, showing his desperation. This creates a tragic tone, as the audience knows he cannot escape what has been decided for him.

A Christmas Carol

Key Quotes (Interactive Flashcards)

Tap any card to reveal its meaning. Use these quotes in your essays!

"Solitary as an oyster."
Theme: Isolation, potential for change
Simile showing Scrooge is closed off, but hints at a "pearl" inside ‚Äì potential for transformation.
"Are there no prisons?"
Theme: Selfishness, ignorance of the poor
Scrooge's cold response to charity collectors. Shows his lack of compassion for suffering.
"I will honour Christmas in my heart."
Theme: Transformation, redemption
Scrooge's promise after his transformation. Shows he has learned to value generosity and community.
"Tiny Tim ‚Ä¶ hoped people saw him."
Theme: Innocence, suffering, moral lesson
Despite his disability, Tim wants to remind people of Jesus who helped the lame walk. Shows innocence and moral purpose.
"This boy is Ignorance. This girl is Want."
Theme: Societal warning
The Ghost of Christmas Present reveals society's failures. Dickens warns of the dangers of ignoring poverty.
"A merry Christmas to everybody!"
Theme: Joy, community spirit
Scrooge's joyful greeting after transformation. Contrasts with his earlier coldness and shows genuine change.

Model Paragraph (Scrooge's Transformation)

Dickens presents Scrooge's transformation as essential for personal and social redemption. Early in the novel, he is described as "solitary as an oyster", suggesting he is closed and hardened. The metaphor hints there is a 'pearl' inside him, foreshadowing his change. By the end, his joyful declaration, "I will honour Christmas in my heart", shows he has embraced generosity and compassion.

üìñ Important Context

Romeo & Juliet - Elizabethan Era

Written: 1594-1596

Key Context: Fate was hugely important in Shakespeare's time. People believed their lives were controlled by the stars. The family feud reflects real Italian vendetta culture.

Why it matters: Shows why audiences would see the tragedy as inevitable, not just bad luck.

A Christmas Carol - Victorian England

Written: 1843

Key Context: Huge gap between rich and poor. No welfare state - poor people went to workhouses (horrible places). Child labor was common.

Why it matters: Dickens wanted to shame wealthy people into helping the poor. The book was meant to create social change.
